## Baha'i Principles Framework

The following 12 principles guide ethical evaluation and decision-making:

### 1. Unity of God
**Principle**: Recognition of one ultimate reality and source of truth  
**Application**: Seek coherent, integrated solutions; avoid fragmented thinking; honor underlying unity in diverse approaches

### 2. Unity of Religion
**Principle**: All great faiths share common spiritual foundation  
**Application**: Respect diverse worldviews; find common ground; recognize wisdom from all traditions

### 3. Unity of Humanity
**Principle**: All people are one human family  
**Application**: Design for global accessibility; consider impact on all communities; reject exclusionary practices

### 4. Equality of Men and Women
**Principle**: Full equality of genders in rights and opportunities  
**Application**: Ensure gender-inclusive design; challenge bias in AI training data; audit for gender equity

### 5. Elimination of All Forms of Prejudice
**Principle**: Active work to remove bias and discrimination  
**Application**: Identify algorithmic bias; test for fairness across demographics; inclusive user research

### 6. Universal Compulsory Education
**Principle**: Education as fundamental right for all  
**Application**: Design for learnability; provide clear documentation; lower barriers to understanding

### 7. Harmony of Science and Religion
**Principle**: Science and faith are complementary paths to truth  
**Application**: Balance empirical evidence with human values; integrate data with wisdom; respect both reason and intuition

### 8. Independent Investigation of Truth
**Principle**: Each person must seek truth for themselves  
**Application**: Provide transparency; avoid manipulation; enable informed choice; respect user autonomy

### 9. World Peace as Practical Goal
**Principle**: Work toward global cooperation and justice  
**Application**: Build bridges between communities; design for collaboration; avoid adversarial patterns

### 10. Universal Auxiliary Language
**Principle**: Shared communication enables understanding  
**Application**: Clear, accessible language in interfaces; i18n/l10n support; plain language over jargon

### 11. Spiritual Solution to Economic Problems
**Principle**: Material prosperity must serve spiritual and social development  
**Application**: Measure success beyond profit; consider long-term human impact; sustainable business models

### 12. A World Federation/Commonwealth
**Principle**: Global governance structures for collective good  
**Application**: Design for interoperability; contribute to commons; open standards over lock-in

## Key Practices

### Consultation Method
**From Baha'i Writings**: "The maturity of the gift of understanding is made manifest through consultation."

- **Prepare**: Reflect individually before group consultation
- **Present**: Share perspective humbly, detached from ego
- **Listen**: Hear others fully, seek to understand
- **Synthesize**: Ideas belong to the group, not individuals
- **Decide**: Reach consensus through principled reasoning
- **Act**: Implement decision wholeheartedly

### Ethical Decision Framework

1. **Identify Stakes**: Who is affected? What are potential harms/benefits?
2. **Apply Principles**: Which Baha'i principles are relevant?
3. **Consult**: Gather diverse perspectives through consultation
4. **Reason**: Evaluate options against principles and consequences
5. **Decide**: Choose path that best serves humanity
6. **Document**: Record reasoning for transparency and learning
7. **Review**: Reassess as context evolves

### Service Orientation
- **Start with Why**: Does this serve genuine human need?
- **Check Assumptions**: Are we projecting our biases onto users?
- **Include Margins**: Have we heard from underrepresented groups?
- **Measure Impact**: How do we know we're helping, not harming?
